Featured Case Study

Hair Oil E-commerce Website

Designing a clear and trustworthy shopping experience for an everyday hair care product.

Role UX/UI Designer
Timeline 2 weeks
Type Concept / Portfolio Project
Project Overview

This concept was created to explore how a simple hair oil product website could feel more approachable, trustworthy, and easy to shop. The main goal was to make the product easy to understand at a glance while keeping the buying experience clear from landing page to checkout.

The Problem

Hair product websites often rely on crowded claims, heavy visuals, or vague benefit language. That can make shoppers hesitate. For this project, the challenge was to present the product with enough clarity and trust signals that users could quickly understand what it is, why it matters, and how to buy it.

Objectives
Improve product clarity Build trust through simple presentation Reduce friction in the purchase flow Create a mobile-friendly experience
Mockup Space

Placeholder frames are included below for homepage, product detail, and cart/checkout screens. These can be swapped with final mockups later without changing the layout structure.

UX Process

The process was kept practical and focused on making the experience easier to understand, trust, and complete.

Define

Clarified the project as an everyday e-commerce experience focused on trust, usability, and straightforward product messaging.

Research

Reviewed common patterns in beauty and hair care sites to see where product claims became too cluttered or hard to trust.

Structure

Mapped the content hierarchy so users could move from product understanding to confidence-building details and then purchase.

Wireframe

Created simple wireframes to test page flow, benefit placement, and the order of trust-building content blocks.

Visual Design

Used a clean, approachable layout with gentle contrast and clear spacing to keep the shopping experience calm and readable.

Cart + Checkout Flow

Kept the path to purchase minimal, focusing on easy review, straightforward actions, and fewer opportunities for confusion.

Reflection

Evaluated how well the layout supported trust and clarity, then noted where the experience could be pushed further with real testing.

Information Hierarchy / User Flow
Landing Understand Product Build Trust Add to Cart Checkout
Key Design Decisions

Simple hero for fast comprehension

The first screen focuses on what the product is and why it matters without unnecessary visual clutter.

Benefits section placed early

Users get quick value cues before needing to commit to deeper reading.

Ingredient transparency for trust

Clear ingredient presentation supports credibility and helps users feel more informed before buying.

Frictionless cart and checkout flow

Cart and checkout interactions were simplified to support a smoother path from interest to purchase.

Clean mobile-first layout

The structure was designed to stay readable and usable on smaller screens where most casual product browsing happens.

BDAI Highlight

I lightly explored a Behavior-Driven Adaptive Interface idea here by using subtle motion and soft CTA emphasis to guide attention without overwhelming the product experience.

Final Outcome

The final concept presents the product with more clarity, builds trust through cleaner structure, and supports a more conversion-friendly shopping flow from landing to checkout.

Reflection

This project strengthened how I think about balancing clarity, trust, and usability inside a small e-commerce experience. If I continued it, I would add more real user feedback, test the mobile purchase flow further, and refine the product detail hierarchy even more.
